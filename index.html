About

Fashion should feel like a conversation, not a private club.

I'm a Nigerian-American fashion creator, stylist, and storyteller working between the DMV and New York. My style didn't come from privilege, it came from attention: to culture, family, history, art, everyday people, and the details others overlook.

I believe you cannot buy an eye. You build one through everything you've lived, loved, questioned, and noticed. That's the lens my content lives by, curate, don't collect. Style is not defined by price, it's revealed through choice, and high and low can exist in the same wardrobe.

For my audience, fashion feels like a conversation, not a private club. I want people to develop their own point of view, change their minds, and dress with more intention, without needing credentials or an expensive closet to do it.

Collaboration Concepts

How I'd approach a first project

Concepts, not completed campaigns, a few directions I'd bring to a new partnership.

01

The Getting-Ready Story

A hotel room, suite, or venue becomes the backdrop for a cinematic getting-dressed narrative, styled entirely around one product or look.

02

One Piece, Three Moments

A single product, a bag, a coat, a pair of shoes, styled across three real-life occasions in one day: an arrival, a daytime stop, an evening out.

03

The Collection As Story

A capsule or collection interpreted as a short narrative moving through a single day or season, rather than a straight lookbook.
